---
title: PWA-1484
description: PWA 1484 is a second-generation single-crystal nickel-based superalloy developed by Pratt & Whitney for high-pressure turbine blades in advanced gas turbine engines. It incorporates rhenium (Re) to enhance high-temperature creep resistance and thermal stability, representing a significant advancement over first-generation single-crystal alloys. The alloy is strengthened by a high volume fraction of γ' (gamma prime) precipitates (Ni₃Al-based) and designed to operate at temperatures up to 1,100°C with thermal barrier coatings (TBCs).  PWA 1484 was developed in the 1980s–1990s as part of Pratt & Whitney’s second-generation single-crystal superalloys. Its introduction of rhenium enabled turbine inlet temperatures exceeding 1,500°C (with TBCs) in engines like the PW4000, significantly improving fuel efficiency and thrust in commercial and military aviation. While surpassed by third-gen alloys (e.g., PWA 1497), PWA 1484 remains a cornerstone of modern turbine blade technology.  The desulfurized version of this alloy is PWA 1429.

## Applications

### Applications of PWA 1429 include:

- Aerospace Gas Turbines:

  - Combustor liners and nozzle guide vanes.
  - High-pressure turbine (HPT) blades and vanes (e.g., PW4000, F119 engines)..

- Rotor blades in land-based power generators.
