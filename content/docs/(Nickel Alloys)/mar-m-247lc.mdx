---
title: MAR-M-247LC
description: MAR-M-247LC is a low-carbon variant of the MAR-M-247 nickel-based superalloy, optimized for improved weldability and reduced susceptibility to carbide-related issues while retaining high-temperature strength. It is a cast, precipitation-hardened alloy used in gas turbine engines and aerospace applications. MAR-M-247LC was developed as a modification of MAR-M-247 (introduced in the 1970s) to address weldability challenges in turbine blade repair. Its low-carbon design makes it a preferred choice for components requiring post-casting repairs or coatings. While newer single-crystal alloys (e.g., CMSX-4, René N5) now dominate high-efficiency engines, MAR-M-247LC remains relevant in industrial turbines and legacy aerospace systems.

## Applications

### Applications of MAR-M-247LC include:

- Aerospace Gas Turbines:

  - Turbine blades and vanes.
  - Combustor components.
  - High-pressure turbine (HPT) sections in jet engines.

- Rotors and seals in power generation systems.

## Properties

- γ (gamma) matrix: Nickel-rich FCC phase.
- γ' Precipitates: ~50–60% volume fraction (Ni₃Al/Ti/Ta).
- Carbides: Reduced MC carbides (TaC, HfC) due to lower carbon.
- Grain Boundary Strengthening: Hf and B segregate to boundaries.
- Density: ~8.5–8.7 g/cm³.
- Oxidation Resistance: Forms protective Al₂O₃ scales.
- Hot Corrosion Resistance: Moderate (Cr content aids resistance).
- Melting Range: ~1,315–1,370°C.
- Thermal Expansion: ~14.5 × 10⁻⁶ /°C (20–1,000°C).
- Thermal Conductivity: ~11–13 W/m·K.
- Service Temperature: Up to 980°C (with coatings).

**Mechanical properties:**

| Property         | Value(Room Temp)               | Value(Elevated Temp 870°C) |
| ---------------- | ------------------------------ | -------------------------- |
| Tensile Strength | 900-1000 MPa                   | 600–700 MPa                |
| Yield Strength   | 800-900 MPa                    | 500–600 MPa                |
| Elongation       | 3-8%                           | 5-10%                      |
| Creep Rupture    | 200 MPa at 1,000 hours @ 850°C | NA                         |

**Advantages**

- High-Temperature Strength: Retains mechanical properties up to 980°C.
- Improved Weldability: Low carbon reduces cracking during repairs.
- Creep Resistance: Suitable for long-term high-stress applications.
- Oxidation Resistance: Performs well in oxidizing environments.

**Limitations**

- Low Ductility: Brittle compared to wrought superalloys.
- Anisotropic Properties: Cast components may have directional weaknesses.
- Cost: Expensive due to Hf, Ta, and Co content.
