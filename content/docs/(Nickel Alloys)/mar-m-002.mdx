title: Mar-M-002
description: Mar-M-002 is a nickel-cobalt-based superalloy designed for extreme temperature and stress environments, particularly in aerospace and industrial gas turbine applications. It is a cast nickel-cobalt superalloy strengthened by a combination of gamma prime (γ') precipitates and solid-solution hardening. This alloy retains structural integrity at temperatures up to 1,000°C for 1,000 hours under 138 MPa stress, making it ideal for high-temperature components

## Applications

### Applications of INCO-713LC include:

- Aerospace: Turbine blades, vanes, and integral discs in military and civil jet engines.
- Fuel/oxidizer turbo-pump components in rocket engines.
- Power Generation: Industrial gas turbine hot-section components (e.g., nozzles, wheels).
- Defense & Automotive: Turbocharger wheels and nuclear reactor components.

## Properties

**Unique features:**

- Casting: Primary method for producing turbine blades and complex geometries.
- Post-Weld Heat Treatment: Required to mitigate residual stresses in welded assemblies.
  cast conditions, refined through heat treatment.

**Mechanical properties:**

- Yield Strength: 770–950 MPa at RT, maintaining ~40% at 1,000°C.
- Tensile Strength: 900–1,100 MPa (RT), 138 MPa retained at 1,000°C under 1,000-hour creep.
- Elongation: 7–13% (RT), demonstrating ductility despite high γ' volume fraction.
- Fatigue Strength: 360–550 MPa at 10⁷ cycles (stress ratio = -1).
- Fracture Toughness: 60.5–67.2 MPa·√m (prevents crack propagation).
- Hardness: 300–450 HV (Vickers).

**Thermal properties:**

- Melting Range: 1,320–1,370°C.
- Max Service Temp: 1,000°C (1,000-hour lifespan at 138 MPa stress).
- Thermal Conductivity: 12–13.5 W/m·°C (limits thermal distortion).
- Thermal Expansion: 11–12 µm/m·°C (20–1,000°C).
- Oxidation Resistance: Forms protective Al₂O₃/Cr₂O₃ scales up to 1,100°.

**Physical properties:**

- High-Temperature Strength: Maintains 138 MPa stress at 1,000°C2.
- Oxidation Resistance: Protective chromium and aluminum oxides enhance durability in oxidizing environments.
- Weldability: Prone to liquation and solidification cracking due to a wide solidification range. Advanced electron beam welding with preheating and stress management via finite element analysis (FEA) enables crack-free joints.
