---
title: PWA 1426
description: PWA 1426 is a rhenium-enhanced nickel-based superalloy optimized for single-crystal (SX) turbine blades and vanes. It is designed to withstand extreme temperatures and mechanical stresses in modern gas turbine engines. PWA 1426 likely represents Pratt & Whitney’s progression toward 3rd/4th-generation superalloys, targeting higher turbine inlet temperatures for improved fuel efficiency and thrust in engines like the PW1100G-JM (Geared Turbofan). Its rhenium content aligns with industry trends to push operational limits while balancing cost and performance.

## Applications

### Applications of PWA 1426 include:

- Aerospace Gas Turbines:

  - Combustor liners and nozzle guide vanes.
  - High-pressure turbine (HPT) blades in jet engines (e.g., Pratt & Whitney’s PW1000G, F135).

- Rotor blades in land-based power generators.

## Properties

- Density: ~8.9–9.2 g/cm³ (higher due to Re/W content).
- Oxidation Resistance: Forms protective Al₂O₃ scales.
- Fatigue Resistance: Excellent due to single-crystal structure.
- Melting Range: ~1,320–1,380°C.
- Thermal Expansion: ~14 × 10⁻⁶ /°C (20–1,000°C).
- Service Temperature: Up to 1,150°C (with thermal barrier coatings).
- Single-crystal γ matrix: No grain boundaries, minimizing creep failure.
- γ' Precipitates: ~60–70% volume fraction (Ni₃Al/Ta/Re).
- TCP Phase Suppression: Re and W additions inhibit harmful topologically close-packed phases.

**Mechanical properties:**

| Property         | Value(Room Temp)               |
| ---------------- | ------------------------------ |
| Tensile Strength | 1300-1600 MPa                  |
| Yield Strength   | 1100-1400 MPa                  |
| Elongation       | 8-12%                          |
| Creep Rupture    | 300 MPa at 1,000 hours @ 980°C |

**Advantages**

- Superior Creep Resistance: Rhenium reduces atomic diffusion at high temperatures.
- Enhanced Temperature Capability: Operates 50–100°C hotter than Re-free alloys.
- Fatigue Resistance: Single-crystal structure withstands thermal cycling.
- Coating Compatibility: Works with advanced TBC systems for higher efficiency.

**Limitations**

- High Cost: Rhenium (~$4,000/kg) significantly increases material expenses.
- Density: Heavier than earlier-generation alloys, impacting weight-sensitive applications.
- Anisotropic Behavior: Mechanical properties vary with crystal orientation.
