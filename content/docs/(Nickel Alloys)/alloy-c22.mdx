Its nominal composition includes 56% nickel, 22% chromium, 13% molybdenum, and smaller amounts of tungsten, iron, and cobalt.

## Applications

#### Alloy C22 finds applications in various industries, including:

- Chemical processing: Used in reactors, heat exchangers, and columns
- Pharmaceutical and biotech: Employed in active pharmaceutical ingredients (API) production, buffer solutions, and water systems
- Waste management: Utilized in waste disposal and incineration scrubber systems
- Power generation: Applied in flue gas desulfurization systems
- Oil and gas: Used in corrosive environments within the industry
- Food and beverage processing: Employed in equipment handling corrosive substances
- Pulp and paper: Used in bleach plants and other corrosive processes

---

## Properties

- Outstanding corrosion resistance to both oxidizing and reducing media
- Excellent resistance to pitting, crevice corrosion, and stress corrosion cracking
- High resistance to oxidizing aqueous media, including wet chlorine and mixtures containing nitric acid or oxidizing acids with chloride ions
- Good fabricability and weldability
