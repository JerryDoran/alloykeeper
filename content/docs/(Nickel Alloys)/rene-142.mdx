---
title: Rene-142
description: René 142 is a second-generation single-crystal nickel-based superalloy developed for high-temperature turbine blade applications in aerospace and industrial gas turbines. Known for its exceptional creep resistance and oxidation stability, it is widely used in advanced jet engines and power-generation systems. René 142 was developed in the 1990s by General Electric (GE) as part of the second generation of rhenium-containing single-crystal superalloys. It enabled significant increases in turbine inlet temperatures (~1,500°C with TBCs) and fuel efficiency in engines like the GE90 and GEnx. While newer alloys (e.g., 3rd-gen René N6) now push performance further, René 142 remains a benchmark for high-temperature reliability.

## Applications

### Applications of RENE 142 include:

- Aerospace Gas Turbines:

  - Combustor components.
  - High-pressure turbine (HPT) blades and vanes.

- Rotor blades in land-based power generators.
- Critical components in high-thrust engines (e.g., Military Jet Engines such as GE F414, F110).

## Properties

- Single-crystal γ matrix: No grain boundaries, reducing creep failure points.
- γ' Precipitates: ~70% volume fraction (Ni₃Al/Ta/Re) for strength.
- Carbides: Minimal due to low carbon content.
- TCP Phase Resistance: Rhenium and W suppress harmful topologically close-packed phases.
- Melting Range: ~1,320–1,370°C.
- Thermal Expansion: ~14.5 × 10⁻⁶ /°C (20–1,000°C).
- Thermal Conductivity: ~10–12 W/m·K.
- Service Temperature: Up to 1,100°C (with thermal barrier coatings).
- Density: ~8.9–9.1 g/cm³.
- Oxidation Resistance: Forms protective Al₂O₃ scales.
- Hot Corrosion Resistance: Good in sulfur-rich environments (Cr content).

**Mechanical properties:**

| Property         | Value(Room Temp)               | Value(Elevated Temp 870°C) |
| ---------------- | ------------------------------ | -------------------------- |
| Tensile Strength | 1200-1400 MPa                  | 800-900 MPa                |
| Yield Strength   | 1000-1200 MPa                  | 700-800 MPa                |
| Elongation       | 8-12%                          | 10-15%                     |
| Creep Rupture    | 250 MPa at 1,000 hours @ 980°C | NA                         |

**Advantages**

- Superior Creep Resistance: Outperforms first-gen alloys (e.g., René N4) by ~30%.
- Oxidation Stability: Maintains integrity in aggressive combustion environments.
- Fatigue Resistance: Excellent low-cycle fatigue (LCF) performance.
- Coating Compatibility: Works with advanced TBC systems for higher temperature operation.

**Limitations**

- High Cost: Rhenium (~$4,000/kg) and single-crystal processing drive expenses.
- Anisotropic Properties: Directional dependence of mechanical properties.
- Weldability: Limited repairability due to single-crystal structure.
