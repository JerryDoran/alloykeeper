## Applications

### Applications of Siemet include:

- Aerospace Gas Turbines:

  - Turbine blades
  - High-pressure vanes
  - Combustor components

- Industrial Gas Turbines:

  - Hot-section components exposed to extreme temperatures.

- Other high-temperature structural applications requiring excellent creep and fatigue resistance.

## Properties

- Melting Range: Likely ~1,300–1,400°C
- Thermal Expansion Coefficient: ~14 x 10⁻⁶ /°C (20–1,000°C)
- Thermal Conductivity: ~12–14 W/m·K
- Service Temperature: Up to 1,100°C with coatings.
- Excellent oxidation and hot corrosion resistance due to chromium and aluminum content.
- High thermal stability through γ' precipitate strengthening.
- Directional solidification or single-crystal casting for improved creep properties.
- Investment casting for complex geometries.

**Advantages**

- High strength at elevated temperatures.
- Excellent creep resistance for long-term service life.
- Superior oxidation and hot corrosion resistance.
- Stable microstructure under thermal cycling conditions.

**Limitations**

- Likely high manufacturing costs due to complex processing requirements.
- Challenging machinability due to high strength and hardness.
- Limited weldability; repairs may require specialized techniques.
