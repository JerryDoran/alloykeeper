---
title: Rene-80
description: René 80 is a precipitation-hardenable nickel-based superalloy developed by General Electric in the 1960s. It was designed to meet the demanding requirements of high-temperature applications, particularly in gas turbine engines. René 80's balanced composition and optimized microstructure make it a versatile alloy for high-temperature applications, offering a good combination of strength, creep resistance, and oxidation resistance. Its properties can be further tailored through precise control of the heat treatment process to meet specific application requirements.

## Applications

### Applications of Rene 80 include:

- Gas turbine engines: Widely used for turbine blades and other hot section components.
- Industrial gas turbines: Applied in power generation systems.
- High-temperature environments: Suitable for applications requiring strength and durability at elevated temperatures.

---

## Properties

- Fabricability: Can be cast and heat-treated, but considered difficult to weld or machine due to high strength.
- Heat treatment: Involves solution annealing followed by aging, which results in the formation of primary and secondary γ' precipitates

**Mechanical properties:**

- Temperature capability: Maintains high mechanical properties at elevated temperatures, with rupture stress of 160 MPa for 100h and 105 MPa for 1000h at 980°C
- Tensile strength: 1158 MPa
- Yield strength: 1034 MPa
- Elongation: 15%
- Hardness: 335 HB

**Microstructure:**

- Features γ (gamma) matrix strengthened by γ' (gamma prime) precipitates
- Bimodal structure consisting of cuboidal primary γ' and spheroidal secondary γ'
- Contains MC/M6C carbides and borides

**Physical properties:**

- Density: 7.7-8.03 g/cm³
- Elastic modulus: 190-210 GPa
- Poisson's ratio: 0.27-0.30
