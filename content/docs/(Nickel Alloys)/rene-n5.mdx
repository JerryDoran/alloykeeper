---
title: Rene-N5
description: René N5 is a second-generation single-crystal nickel-based superalloy developed by GE Aviation for high-pressure turbine blades in advanced gas turbine engines. It incorporates rhenium (Re) to enhance high-temperature creep resistance and thermal stability, representing a significant advancement over first-generation single-crystal alloys.  René N5 was developed in the 1990s as part of GE Aviation's second-generation single-crystal superalloys. Its introduction of rhenium enabled higher turbine inlet temperatures (~1,500°C with TBCs) in engines like the GE90, significantly improving fuel efficiency and thrust in commercial aviation. While surpassed by third-gen alloys (e.g., René N6), René N5 remains a cornerstone of modern gas turbine technology.

## Applications

### Applications of RENE N5 include:

- Aerospace Gas Turbines:

  - Turbine vanes and combustor liners.
  - High-pressure turbine (HPT) blades in jet engines (e.g., GE90, GEnx).

- Rotor blades in land-based power generators.
