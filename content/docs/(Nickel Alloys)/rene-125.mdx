---
title: Rene-125
description: René 125 is a nickel-based polycrystalline superalloy developed for high-temperature applications in gas turbine engines. It combines excellent creep resistance, oxidation resistance, and thermal stability, making it suitable for critical rotating components. René 125 is a precipitation-hardened, cast nickel-based superalloy strengthened by γ' (gamma prime) precipitates. The alloy retains strength up to 980°C (1,800°F) and is optimized for turbine blades, vanes, and disks. René 125 was developed in the 1980s as an improvement over earlier polycrystalline alloys (e.g., René 77, IN-738). Its optimized composition extended the service life of turbine components in both aerospace and industrial gas turbines. While newer single-crystal alloys now dominate high-pressure turbine blades, René 125 remains widely used for rotating components requiring a balance of strength, cost, and manufacturability.

## Applications

### Applications of Rene-125 include:

- Aerospace Gas Turbines:

  - High-pressure turbine(HPT) blades.
  - Turbine disks and seals.

- Rotor components in jet engines.
- Combustor liners and exhaust systems.

## Properties

- Density: ~8.3–8.5 g/cm³.
- Oxidation Resistance: Forms protective Cr₂O₃/Al₂O₃ scales.
- Hot Corrosion Resistance: Good in sulfur-rich environments.
- Melting Range: ~1,300–1,350°C.
- Thermal Expansion: ~13.5 × 10⁻⁶ /°C (20–1,000°C).
- Thermal Conductivity: ~11–13 W/m·K.
- Service Temperature: Up to 980°C (with coatings).
- γ (gamma) matrix: Nickel-rich FCC phase.
- γ' Precipitates: ~50–60% volume fraction (Ni₃Al/Ti/Ta).
- Carbides: MC (TaC, TiC) and M₂₃C₆ (Cr-rich) at grain boundaries.
- Grain Boundary Strengthening: Boron and zirconium segregate to grain boundaries.

**Mechanical properties:**

| Property         | Value(Room Temp)               |
| ---------------- | ------------------------------ |
| Tensile Strength | 1,200–1,400 MPa                |
| Yield Strength   | 950–1,100 MPa                  |
| Elongation       | 8-15%                          |
| Creep Rupture    | 200 MPa at 1,000 hours @ 850°C |

**Advantages**

- High creep resistance at elevated temperatures.
- Balanced oxidation and hot corrosion resistance.
- Thermal fatigue resistance due to polycrystalline grain structure.
- Cost-effective compared to single-crystal alloys.

**Limitations**

- Lower temperature capability than single-crystal alloys (e.g., CMSX-4).
- Anisotropic properties in cast components.
- Susceptibility to TCP phases (topologically close-packed phases) after prolonged exposure.
