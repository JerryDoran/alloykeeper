---
title: Rene N2
description: René N2 is a third-generation single-crystal nickel-based superalloy developed by GE Aviation for advanced gas turbine engines. Known for its exceptional high-temperature strength, creep resistance, and oxidation stability, it is a key material in modern aerospace and industrial turbines.  René N2 was developed in the late 1990s/early 2000s by GE Aviation as part of the third-generation superalloys.

## Applications

### Applications of RENE N2 include:

- Aerospace: Turbine blades, vanes, or combustor components in early jet engines.
- Industrial Turbines: Stationary blades or seals in power-generation systems.
- Nuclear/Energy: High-temperature fasteners or reactor components.
