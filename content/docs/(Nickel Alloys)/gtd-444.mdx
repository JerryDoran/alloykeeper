---
title: GTD-444
description: GTD-444 is a directionally solidified (DS) nickel-based superalloy developed for high-temperature applications in industrial gas turbines. GTD-444's optimization for directional solidification and large-part castability, combined with its high-temperature strength and creep resistance, makes it valuable for critical components in industrial gas turbines. As of January 31, 2008, GTD-444 alloy in the advanced machine fleet leader had passed 8,000 fired hours, considered an industry milestone

## Applications

### Applications of GTD-444 include:

- Derived from the single crystal (SC) René N4 alloy
- Optimized for directional solidification and large-part castabilit
- Industrial gas turbines: Used in GE's steam-cooled MS7001H (7H) for 3rd and 4th stage turbine buckets.
- Conventionally cooled gas turbines: Applied in MS7001FB (7FB) and MS9001FB (9FB) for 2nd and 3rd stage turbine buckets.
- Maintains strength and oxidation resistance at elevated temperatures.
- Suitable for use in hot sections of gas turbines.

---

## Properties

- Designed for directional solidification casting
- Can produce large castings up to ~36 inches (91 cm) long and ~40 pounds (18 kg) in weight

**Mechanical properties:**

- Exhibits improved creep and fatigue properties compared to DS GTD-111.

  **Microstructure:**

- Features γ (gamma) matrix strengthened by γ' (gamma prime) precipitates.
- Contains carbides for additional strengthening.
