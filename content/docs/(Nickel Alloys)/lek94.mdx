---
title: LEK-94
description: LEK-94 is an advanced nickel-based superalloy designed for high-temperature applications, particularly in aerospace and gas turbine engines. LEK-94 is a single-crystal, precipitation-hardened nickel-based superalloy strengthened by γ' (gamma prime) precipitates. LEK-94's combination of high-temperature strength, creep resistance, and relatively low density makes it an excellent choice for advanced aerospace and industrial gas turbine applications where performance under extreme conditions is crucial.

## Properties

**Unique features:**

- Shear creep behavior studied at temperatures between 980 and 1050°C and shear stresses between 50 and 140 MPa.
- Apparent activation energy for creep: Q_app = 466 kJ/mol.
- Norton-law stress exponent: n = 61
- Microstructural evolution during creep includes rafting, γ channel dislocation filling, and formation of dislocation networks at γ/γ' interfaces.
- Surface tension and viscosity measured using the Oscillating Drop Method during parabolic flights.
- Thermophysical properties (surface tension, viscosity, heat capacity, total hemispherical emissivity, specific resistivity, and mass density) studied using electromagnetic levitation on the International Space Station.
- Excellent high-temperature strength
- Superior creep resistance
- Low density
- Good toughness

**Thermal properties:**

- Thermal Expansion: 14.3 μm/m-°C (20-600°C).
- Melting Range: Solidus and liquidus temperatures not specified, but likely similar to other Ni-based superalloys (around 1300-1400°C).
