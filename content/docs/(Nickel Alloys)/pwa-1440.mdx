---
title: PWA-1440
description: PWA 1480 is a first-generation single-crystal nickel-based superalloy developed by Pratt & Whitney for use in high-temperature gas turbine applications. Designed for exceptional creep resistance and thermal stability, it is primarily used in turbine blades and vanes.  PWA 1440 was among the first single-crystal superalloys commercialized in the 1980s, enabling significant increases in turbine entry temperatures and engine efficiency. Its development paved the way for advanced alloys like PWA 1484 (2nd-gen) and PWA 1497 (3rd-gen), which incorporate rhenium for enhanced performance.

## Applications

### Applications of PWA-1440 include:

- Aerospace Gas Turbines:

  - High-pressure turbine blades and vanes.
  - Combustor liners.

- Industrial Gas Turbines:

  - Rotors and hot-section components.

- Power Generation:
  - Turbine blades in land-based turbines.

## Properties

- Single-crystal γ matrix: Eliminates grain boundaries, reducing creep failure points.
- γ' Precipitates: ~60–70% volume fraction (Ni₃Al/Ti/Ta) for strength.
- Carbides: TaC and HfC at interdendritic regions for grain boundary strengthening.
- Density: ~8.7–8.9 g/cm³.
- Oxidation Resistance: Forms protective Cr₂O₃/Al₂O₃ scales.
- Hot Corrosion Resistance: Performs well in sulfur-rich environments.
- Melting Range: ~1,300–1,400°C.
- Thermal Expansion: ~14 × 10⁻⁶ /°C (20–1,000°C).
- Thermal Conductivity: ~10–12 W/m·K.
- Service Temperature: Up to 980°C (with thermal barrier coatings).

**Mechanical properties:**

| Property         | Value(Room Temp)               | Elevated Temp(980 C) |
| ---------------- | ------------------------------ | -------------------- |
| Tensile Strength | 1,000–1,200 MPa                | 600-800 Mpa          |
| Yield Strength   | 800–1,000 MPa                  | 500-700 Mpa          |
| Elongation       | ~10%                           | 10-20%               |
| Creep Rupture    | 225 MPa at 1,000 hours @ 980°C | NA                   |

**Advantages**

- Thermal fatigue resistance due to single-crystal structure.
- Excellent creep resistance at high temperatures.
- Superior fatigue resistance due to single-crystal structure.
- Good oxidation and hot corrosion resistance.
- Thermal stability for long-term service.

**Limitations**

- High cost due to single-crystal casting and proprietary processing.
- Limited weldability.
- Anisotropic properties(directionally dependent strength) due to single-crystal structure.
