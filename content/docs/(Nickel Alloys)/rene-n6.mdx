---
title: Rene N6
description: René N6 is a third-generation single-crystal nickel-based superalloy developed by GE Aviation for advanced gas turbine engines. Known for its exceptional high-temperature strength, creep resistance, and oxidation stability, it is a key material in modern aerospace and industrial turbines.  René N6 was developed in the late 1990s/early 2000s by GE Aviation as part of the third-generation superalloys.

## Applications

### Applications of RENE N6 include:

- Aerospace Gas Turbines:

  - Turbine vanes and combustor liners.
  - High-pressure turbine (HPT) blades in jet engines (e.g., GE90, GEnx).

- Rotor blades in land-based power generators.
- Critical components in high-thrust engines (e.g., Military Jet Engines such as F136)
