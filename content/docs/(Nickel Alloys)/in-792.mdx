---
title: IN-792
description: IN 792 is a nickel-based superalloy designed for high-temperature applications, particularly in gas turbine engines. N792's combination of high-temperature strength, creep resistance, and oxidation resistance makes it an excellent choice for advanced gas turbine applications where performance under extreme conditions is crucial. Some limitations of the alloy include complex processing requirements, challenging to machine and weld and higher cost compared to some other superalloys.

## Applications

### Applications of IN-792 include:

- Gas turbine engine components (e.g., turbine blades, vanes).
- Industrial gas turbines.
- Aircraft engine parts.
- High-temperature structural components.
- Petrochemical industry equipment.

---

### Manufacturing & Processing:

- Investment casting for complex geometries.
- Directional solidification for improved creep properties.

- Heat Treatment:

  - Solution treatment: typically 1120°C for 2 hours, air cool.
  - Aging: 845°C for 24 hours, air cool.

- Machining: Challenging due to high strength; often requires specialized tooling and techniques.
- Welding: Limited weldability; often requires special procedures and post-weld heat treatment.

## Properties

**Unique features:**

- High strength at elevated temperatures.
- Excellent creep resistance.
- Good oxidation and hot corrosion resistance.
- Thermal stability.
- Density: ~8.25 g/cm³.
- Excellent oxidation and hot corrosion resistance.
- Good thermal stability.

**Microstructure:**

- γ (gamma) matrix with coherent γ' (gamma prime) precipitates.
- MC carbides (primarily Ti, Ta, and W).
- Possible formation of TCP (Topologically Close-Packed) phases during long-term exposure at high temperatures.

**Physical properties:**

- Tensile Strength (Room Temp): ~1000-1200 MPa.
- Yield Strength (Room Temp): ~800-950 MPa.
- Elongation: ~5-8%.
- Creep Rupture Strength: 248 MPa for 1000 hours at 871°C.
- Fatigue Strength: High resistance to thermal and mechanical fatigue
