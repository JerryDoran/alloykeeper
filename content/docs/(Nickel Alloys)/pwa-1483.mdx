## Properties

- Density: ~8.25 g/cm³.
- Good oxidation and hot corrosion resistance
- Melting Range: ~1260-1315°C.
- Thermal Expansion Coefficient: ~14-15 x 10^-6 /°C (20-800°C)
- Service Temperature: Up to about 950°C.
- γ (gamma) matrix with coherent γ' (gamma prime) precipitates.
- MC carbides (primarily Ti, Ta, and W).

**Mechanical properties:**

| Property         | Value(Room Temp)               |
| ---------------- | ------------------------------ |
| Tensile Strength | 1,000–1,200 MPa                |
| Yield Strength   | 800-950 MPa                    |
| Elongation       | 5-8%                           |
| Creep Rupture    | 248 MPa at 1,000 hours @ 871°C |

**Advantages**

- Good strength at elevated temperatures.
- Excellent creep resistance.
- Good oxidation and hot corrosion resistance.
- Balanced composition for optimal mechanical properties.

**Limitations**

- Lower temperature capability compared to later-generation superalloys.
- Complex processing requirements.
- Challenging to machine and weld.
