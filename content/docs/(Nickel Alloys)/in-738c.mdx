## Properties

**High-temperature behavior:**

- High-Temperature Strength: Retains mechanical properties up to 750°C (1382°F), with short-term stability beyond 1000°C (1832°F).
- Creep Resistance: Enhanced resistance to deformation under prolonged stress at high temperatures, critical for turbine blades and combustors.
- Corrosion/Oxidation Resistance: Chromium and aluminum form a protective oxide layer, ideal for marine and acidic environments.
- Fatigue Resistance: Withstands cyclic stresses in aerospace and power generation applications.

**Unique features:**

- Casting: Optimized for large, complex shapes (e.g., turbine blades).
- Heat Treatment: Solution annealing (1150–1200°C) followed by precipitation hardening to refine γ' distribution.
- Additive Manufacturing: Laser Powder Bed Fusion (LPBF) enables crack-free, intricate geometries.

**Microstructure:**

- γ (gamma) matrix strengthened by γ' (gamma prime) precipitates (Ni₃Al/Ti).
- Carbides (MC, M₂₃C₆): Formed with niobium, titanium, and tungsten, enhancing grain boundary strength.
- Dendritic structure in as-cast conditions, refined through heat treatment

**Physical properties:**

- Tensile Strength: 1050–1300 MPa
- Yield Strength: ≥900 MPa
- Elongation: ≥16% (excellent ductility)
- Hardness: ~390 HV
