---
title: Rene 41
description: Rene 41 is a precipitation-hardenable nickel-based superalloy designed for high-temperature applications. Rene 41's combination of high-temperature strength, corrosion resistance, and creep properties makes it invaluable for applications requiring durability in extreme environments, particularly in aerospace and high-performance industries
---

---

## Composition

- Ni: Balance
- Cr: 18.00-20.00%
- Mo: 9.00-10.50%
- Co: 10.00-12.00%
- Al: 1.40-1.80%
- Ti: 3.00-3.30%
- Fe: 5.00% max

---

## Applications

#### Rene 41 finds applications in various industries, including:

- Aerospace: Widely used in jet engine components such as turbine blades, wheels, casings, and afterburner parts.
- Missiles: Applied in various missile components.
- Space exploration: Used in the outer shell of the Mercury space capsule.
- Industrial: Employed in springs, fasteners, and other components requiring high-temperature strength.
- Automotive: Used in high-performance engine components.
- Polystyrene foam manufacturing: Applied in hot-wire foam cutters

---

## Properties

- Melting point: 2400-2500°F (1316-1371°C)
- Density: 0.298 lb/cu in (8249 kg/m³)
- Specific heat capacity: 0.108 Btu/lb-°F (0.452 kJ/kg-K) at 70-600°F (21-316°C)
- Mechanical properties: Offers high tensile strength (170 ksi min), good yield strength (130 ksi min), and excellent creep resistance.
- Corrosion resistance: Provides outstanding resistance to oxidation and corrosion at elevated temperatures, particularly against jet engine combustion gases.
- Fabricability: Can be hot worked, forged, and welded using specific techniques
