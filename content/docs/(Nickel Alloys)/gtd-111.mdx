---
title: GTD-111
description: GTD-111 is a nickel-based superalloy developed by General Electric for advanced gas turbine applications. GTD-111's combination of high-temperature strength, creep resistance, and oxidation resistance makes it an excellent choice for advanced gas turbine applications where performance under extreme conditions is crucial. Its balanced composition also offers good castability, making it suitable for complex component geometries. The alloy maintains structural integrity at temperatures up to about 900°C (1652°F).

## Applications

### Applications of GTD-111 include:

- Gas turbine engine components, particularly:
  - Turbine blades
  - Vanes
  - Nozzles
- Industrial gas turbines.
- Aircraft engine parts.
- High-temperature structural components

---

## Properties

- Investment casting for complex geometries.
- Directional solidification for improved creep properties.
- Heat Treatment:
  - Solution treatment: typically 1120°C for 2 hours, air cool.
  - Aging: 845°C for 24 hours, air cool.
- Machining: Challenging due to high strength; often requires specialized tooling and techniques.
- Welding: Limited weldability; often requires special procedures and post-weld heat treatment.

### Advantages

- High strength at elevated temperatures.
- Excellent creep resistance.
- Good oxidation and hot corrosion resistance.
- Thermal stability.
- Balanced composition for optimal mechanical properties.
- Improved castability compared to some other high-performance superalloys.

### Limitations

- Complex processing requirements.
- Challenging to machine and weld.
- Higher cost compared to some other superalloys.

**Microstructure**:

- γ (gamma) matrix with coherent γ' (gamma prime) precipitates.
- MC carbides (primarily Ti, Ta, and W).
- Possible formation of TCP (Topologically Close-Packed) phases during long-term exposure at high temperatures

**Mechanical Properties**

- Tensile Strength (Room Temp): ~1000-1200 MPa.
- Yield Strength (Room Temp): ~800-950 MPa.
- Elongation: ~5-8%.
- Creep Rupture Strength: 206 MPa for 1000 hours at 871°C.
- Fatigue Strength: High resistance to thermal and mechanical fatigue.
