U500’s combination of high-temperature strength and corrosion resistance makes it indispensable in advanced turbine systems, though its high cobalt content increases cost compared to alternatives like IN718.

## Applications

### Applications of U500 include:

U500 is critical in gas turbine engines and aerospace systems:

- Turbine Disks: High rotational stress and temperature resistance.
- Blades/Vanes: Withstands hot corrosion in jet engines.
- Industrial Gas Turbines: Rotors and combustor components.
- Nuclear Reactors: High-temperature fasteners

## Properties

**Unique features:**

- Casting: Investment casting for complex geometries (e.g., turbine blades).

Heat Treatment:

- Solution Treatment: 1,080–1,120°C followed by rapid cooling.
- Aging: 650–760°C to precipitate γ' phase9.
- Hot Workability: Forged above γ' solvus (1,100–1,170°C) for fine grain structure

**Mechanical properties:**

| Property         | Value(Room Temp) | Value @ 700 C |
| ---------------- | ---------------- | ------------- |
| Tensile Strength | 1,200–1,400 MPa  | 800–1,000 MPa |
| Yield Strength   | 950–1,100 MPa    | 700–800 MPa   |
| Elongation       | 12–20%           | 15–25%        |

**Thermal properties:**

- Thermal Expansion: 13.5 µm/m·°C (20–700°C)
- Thermal Conductivity: 11.5 W/m·K (700°C)
- Melting Range: 1,315–1,370°C
